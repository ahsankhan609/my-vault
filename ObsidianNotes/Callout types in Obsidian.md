Let me provide a helpful answer about using warning callouts in Obsidian.**`[!warning]` is a callout type in Obsidian** that creates a styled warning block for highlighting important cautions or alerts in your notes.

## Syntax

```markdown
> [!warning] Optional Title
> This is the warning content
```

## Example

```markdown
> [!warning] Critical Update Required
> Your database needs migration before upgrading to the next version.
```

This renders as a visually distinct box (usually with an orange/yellow border and warning icon) that stands out in your note.

## Other Common Callout Types

- `[!note]` — General information
- `[!tip]` — Helpful suggestions
- `[!important]` — Essential details
- `[!caution]` — Serious warnings
- `[!failure]` or `[!error]` — Something went wrong
- `[!example]` — Example or use case
- `[!quote]` — Cited quote

You can customize the title (or leave it blank), and the styling is configurable via your Obsidian theme.
